01

Mining Exploration

Mining companies face soaring discovery costs and longer project timelines. GeoMiner.AI targets resources more efficiently across all commodities — copper, lithium, gold, rare earths and more. By fusing seismic, geochemical, and topographic data, the platform generates mineral potential heatmaps that highlight high-probability zones, reducing dry drill holes and accelerating new deposit discovery. Executives can expect up to a 40% reduction in site costs and much higher drilling success rates.

Where majors like Rio Tinto use AI to complement traditional geological methods, GeoMiner.AI extends this by automating the entire interpretation workflow — fewer hours on manual interpretation, more time evaluating promising prospects. For critical minerals like lithium and nickel, the platform integrates satellite and drone LiDAR surveys to rapidly map surface expressions of subsurface deposits, even under vegetation.

02

Oil & Gas and Energy

In oil, gas, and renewable energy, GeoMiner.AI accelerates hydrocarbon and critical-mineral exploration. The industry is flooded with legacy seismic data, but traditional analysis is slow — months per 3D volume — and error-prone. Our seismic AI highlights faults and traps in a fraction of the time, cutting well-planning timelines from weeks to seconds while lowering costs and environmental impact.

Renewable energy benefits similarly: GeoMiner.AI processes geophysical surveys and satellite imagery to pinpoint lithium, cobalt, and rare earth elements for batteries and geothermal. For oil & gas majors, fault-detection AI improves reservoir characterization and reduces exploration risk where industry success rates hover around 30–40%. Native support for SEG-Y and LAS data exports cleanly into standard reservoir simulators and GIS.

03

Government & Research

Geological surveys and environmental agencies rely on geospatial data for resource management and hazard mapping. GeoMiner.AI turns growing data libraries into strategic intelligence — supporting land-use planning, earthquake and fault mapping, and climate-related studies. LiDAR-derived 3D models sharpen understanding of fault lines and floodplains, and real-time analysis is valuable for monitoring evolving situations such as active fault detection during a seismic event.

The web-based dashboard and API integration suit public-sector IT, running in secure on-premises, cloud, or air-gapped environments. GeoMiner.AI already supports critical-minerals assessments for energy-transition planning, helping governments surface domestic sources of lithium, copper, and battery metals — paired with enterprise-grade security and flexible SaaS, white-label, or private-cloud deployment.
